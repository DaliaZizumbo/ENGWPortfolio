What I needed to consider

My sources had to meet strict standards: peer reviewed research articles only, and at least 6 of them.

The audience was specifically other researchers in my field.

The style, tone, and arrangement all had to be completely scholarly.

I followed an actual published model lit review for style, arrangement, citation, and tone.

This required me to accurately represent what is being published in the literature instead of trying to prove a hypothesis of my own.

This also required me to carefully synthesize information from multiple sources (and not just summarize them one by one).

My process

Whenever I have a class project, I always try to connect it to my culture since most of my classes are very science, code, or math heavy. That is why I decided to research and write about how Hispanic cultural origins shape health behaviors. I found it really interesting to learn about how culture can influence health behaviors and outcomes, and I wanted to explore this topic in more depth. I started by doing some preliminary research to get a sense of the existing literature on this topic, and then I narrowed down my focus to specific aspects of daily life and wellness that are influenced by Hispanic cultural origins. I also made sure to follow the guidelines for the project closely, and I spent a lot of time revising and editing my paper to make sure it was clear, concise, and well-organized. Overall, I found this project to be a rewarding experience that allowed me to explore a topic that I'm passionate about while also developing my research and writing skills.
