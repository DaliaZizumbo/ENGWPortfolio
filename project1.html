What this project is about

An intellectual autobiography with a focus on my university education in my major — investigating how significant ideas and educational experiences in my field while at NU have been shaped by major theorists, and writing to a specific audience (my peers in the College of Science).

What I needed to consider

Learn things both as I wrote and from my writing.

There was no "right" answer or objectively correct way to approach this. Instead, I needed to come to my own methods, my own voice, and my own description of a knowledge front.

My audience was specifically the other students in my class who are all in the College of Science. This audience allowed and required me to draw on what I learned about my peers as class members, as fellow NU students, and as readers.

Tone, style, arrangement, and look.

My process

When drafting this project, I found it helpful to list the classes I had taken and the concepts I had learned from each. From there, I was able to identify the key themes and ideas that would form the foundation of my paper. After doing this I saw that there was also a connection with my lived experiences and what I had done at my co-op! After identifying these themes, I was able to organize my paper around them, using specific examples from my classes and co-op experience to illustrate my points. I also made sure to revise and edit my paper multiple times to ensure that it was clear, concise, and well-organized.
